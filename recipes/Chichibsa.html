CHICHIBSA

How Chechibsa made of

CHECHIBSA, a traditional Ethiopian dish, is a comforting and hearty meal made from finely broken flatbread mixed with spiced clarified butter (niter kibbeh) and berbere spice. To prepare chechibsa, start by baking a simple flatbread using flour, water, and a pinch of salt. Once baked, tear the flatbread into small, bite-sized pieces. In a pan, melt niter kibbeh, a seasoned clarified butter infused with garlic, ginger, and aromatic spices. Stir in a generous amount of berbere spice to create a rich, flavorful base. Add the broken pieces of flatbread to the pan and mix thoroughly, allowing the bread to absorb the spices and butter. Serve warm, often accompanied by a side of fresh yogurt or honey for added flavor. Chechibsa is a beloved breakfast or snack that embodies the rich culinary traditions of Ethiopia

Ingredients

  • Flatbread (prepared from flour, water, and salt)
  • Niter kibbeh (Ethiopian spiced clarified butter)
  • Optional: Honey or fresh yogurt (for serving)
  • Berbere spice
