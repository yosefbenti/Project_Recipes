BEYAYINET

Beyayinet Description

Beyayinet is a popular Ethiopian mixed platter that offers a delightful variety of vegetarian dishes, typically served on a large injera, a sourdough flatbread made from teff flour. To prepare beyayinet, you need to cook an assortment of dishes, each with its unique flavor. These may include shiro wat (a creamy chickpea stew), misir wat (spicy lentils), atkilt wat (sautéed cabbage, carrots, and potatoes), and gomen (stewed collard greens). Additionally, you can prepare azifa (a tangy green lentil salad) and kik alicha (mild split peas cooked with turmeric). Each dish is cooked separately with traditional Ethiopian spices like berbere, turmeric, and garlic, ensuring distinct flavors. The injera is spread out as a base, and small portions of each dish are spooned onto it in a colorful arrangement. Diners tear pieces of injera to scoop up the various stews, making it a communal and interactive meal. Beyayinet is a vegetarian delight, often enjoyed during fasting periods or by those who appreciate Ethiopian culinary diversity.

Ingredients

  • For Misir Wat (spicy lentils):
    • Red lentils
    • Berbere spice
    • Garlic
    • Onion
    • Oil or niter kibbeh
  • For Shiro Wat (chickpea stew):
    • Shiro powder (ground chickpeas with spices)
    • Garlic
    • Onion
    • Niter kibbeh
  • For Atkilt Wat (sautéed vegetables):
    • Cabbage
    • Carrots
    • Potatoes
    • Turmeric
    • Garlic
    • Oil
  • For Gomen (collard greens):
    • Collard greens
    • Garlic
    • Onion
    • Niter kibbeh
  • For Azifa (lentil salad):
    • Green lentils
    • Mustard
    • Lime juice
    • Onion
    • Green chili
  • Injera (sourdough flatbread made from teff flour)
