DORO

How Doro made of

DORO,Wat is one of Ethiopia's most iconic dishes, a rich and spicy chicken stew often reserved for special occasions and celebrations. To make doro wat, start by preparing a generous amount of berbere spice mix, which forms the heart of the dish. Begin cooking by slowly caramelizing a large quantity of finely chopped onions without oil until deeply golden. Add niter kibbeh (Ethiopian spiced clarified butter) to the onions, followed by minced garlic and ginger, creating a fragrant base. Next, stir in the berbere spice and cook it briefly to enhance its flavor. Add chicken pieces, traditionally bone-in and skin-on, to the pan, coating them in the spice mixture. Pour in some water or chicken stock and simmer the stew slowly, allowing the flavors to meld and the chicken to become tender. Hard-boiled eggs are often added towards the end of cooking, soaking up the flavorful sauce. Doro Wat is served on a large injera, with additional injera pieces used to scoop up the stew. Its deep, spicy flavors and tender chicken make it a centerpiece of Ethiopian cuisine, often enjoyed with family and friends.

Ingredients

  • Chicken (bone-in pieces, skin-on preferred)
  • Hard-boiled eggs
  • Onion (large quantities, finely chopped)
  • Niter kibbeh
  • Berbere spice
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Water or chicken stock
  • Lemon juice (to marinate the chicken)
  • Salt
  • Optional: Cardamom, black pepper (additional spices)
  • Injera (for serving)
